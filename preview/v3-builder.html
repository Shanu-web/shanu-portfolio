How I think about building

Enterprise systems fail not because the architecture was wrong, but because someone skipped the boring parts. Observability. Retries. The 3am log line that explains what just broke. I build for that 3am — production-first, not demo-first.

I call myself a builder because that's the part of the work I keep coming back to. I'm not selling three-day workshops. I'm not writing think-pieces. I ship systems, and I tell other people how I shipped them.

The free hours for students aren't a marketing flywheel. They're a standards thing — when good engineers stop teaching the next ones, the field gets worse. So I keep the door open. That's the whole calculation.

— Shanu, Bengaluru, May 2026
